    • JT Heenan is an elementary and special education major. JT is from Mechanicsburg, Pa. Since beginning his education at West Virginia University, JT has been involved in Mountaineer Maniacs, student conduct board, Residence Hall Council and served as an assistant chair for both Mr. and Ms. Mountaineer and Mountaineer Week. JT is also a current resident assistant in Brooke Tower. JT also loves sports. He plays semi-professional baseball during his summers, has taught life skills swimming to students with special needs, has been a coach for legion baseball, has volunteered with children’s baseball hitting clinics during summers and is an avid weight lifter. JT is running on a platform of College Transition & Support. His goal is to give students all the opportunities and resources available to make for a successful freshman year. The first eight weeks of a student's college career are key for success at WVU. By helping students take the first step toward a new club, tutor, mentor or hobby, that student will soon be better equipped to thrive at the University. JT’s platform also addresses WVU upperclassman involvement. Sometimes when upperclassmen move off campus, they no longer take part in the activities on campus. There are numerous students who live off campus who have great ideas for WVU to make better place. It is never too late to get involved and be heard. In some cases, a first year student who has not yet had success at WVU might lose focus on their primary focus of academics. First semester students need time to lay the foundation for their academics and find their identity at WVU. If freshmen wait until their second semester to pledge into Greek Life, it will make for a stronger academic University.
