Graduate Student Innovation

  • Morgan Leach

    Leadership Experience: President of the Graduate Student Advancement Society (GSAS),Social Chair for his law school class, Assistant Provost for Graduate Student Affairs Graduate Student Advisory Committee

    M0_leach



    • J. Morgan Leach graduated with his bachelor’s degree in public relations from West Virginia University in 2012, and is currently a prospective West Virginia Bar member in his second year at the West Virginia University College of Law, earning a concentration in Energy and Sustainable Development with a focus on environmental law, renewable energy production, natural resource development and international climate change. Leach is a West Virginia native from Parkersburg and is the president of the Graduate Student Advancement Society (GSAS), the social chair for his law school class, and is on the Assistant Provost for Graduate Student Affairs Graduate Student Advisory Committee. His platform is Graduate Student Innovation. As a law student, Morgan has found that graduate students are heavily under-represented in SGA at WVU. He hopes to work with members of the Board to create an entity of SGA that will represent graduate students. Morgan understands that all students have a different college experience and he wants to best represent those different experiences.
