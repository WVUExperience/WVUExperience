Anna Onderik

Retention and Recruitment

  • Anna Onderik

    SGA Experience: One year as an intern and executive director

    Campus Involvement: WVU Sport and Exercise Psychology Club

    AnnaOnderik



    • Anna Onderik is a sophomore sport and exercise psychology major. Anna is originally from Sterling Heights, Michigan (which is near Detroit). Anna chose to come to West Virginia University for her program, but soon fell in love with the campus when she visited in late March of her senior year in high school. In her time at WVU, Anna has been active in the Student Government Association, where she served as an intern and an executive director. Anna is currently the executive director of recruitment and has been working on adding an essay to West Virginia University’s application for admission. During her freshman year, Anna was involved in Residence Hall Association and Sport and the Exercise Psychology Club. Outside of Student Government, Anna danced in two pieces for the Dance program’s Dance Now! Showcase in February. On top of classes, dance practice, and Student Government, Anna still makes time to enjoy college. Anna’s favorite experience, so far, of her sophomore year has been when ESPN’s College Gameday came to WVU for the WVU-TCU football game. Students began what is known as “Tent City” where tents were set up on the Mountainlair Green one week before the game even occurred. By game day, over 200 tents took over the Green and Tent City even had a mayor. Walking through Tent City and experiencing the spirit and pride the students showed to her was an unforgettable experience. Anna’s platform is Recruitment and Retention. Anna would love to expand the Mountaineer family. Creating a promotional video for the University can do just that. By including student testimonials along with basic information, WVU can be marketed for any future student, especially on the Western side of the nation. As the University is a family, losing students through lack of retention is a major issue. Anna seeks to increase the retention rate and address the issues of why students are leaving WVU. Those issues can be financial, cultural, emotional or academic. Anna would also like to add an essay to WVU’s application for admission. Reviewing a student on a holistic level is a better way to assess the student, rather than numbers on a piece of paper.
