  • Iryna Kovtoniuk

    Campus Involvement: International Student Organization and UNICEF Club

    Leadership Experience: Resident Assistant in Dadisman Hall

    irynakovtoniuk



    • Iryna Kovtoniuk is a sophomore majoring in finance in the College of Business & Economics at West Virginia University. Iryna was born and raised in the Ukraine, and at the age of 16 was granted an opportunity to be an exchange student in the U.S, she lived with a family in a small town in Arkansas. In August of 2013, Iryna began her college education in Morgantown as an international student. This campus has become a huge part of Iryna’s life, not only because as a student she spends a majority of her time here, but also because WVU has that special, Gold & Blue spirit in it – spirit that lets you know you can do whatever you set your mind to. Iryna has been involved on campus since the beginning of her time at WVU. She has been a member of the International Student Organization, is a member the UNICEF Club and she currently works as a resident assistant in Dadisman Hall. Iryna knows that all of her experiences will help her to reach her primary goal – to make WVU a better place. Her favorite experience at WVU was cooking 300 Ukrainian-style stuffed pancakes for an international dinner last spring. She also remembers singing “Country Roads” in the Coliseum during the New Student Welcome in 2013, as one of her brightest memories. Iryna’s platform is International Family and Community. The goal of this platform is to promote integration of international students into WVU community as a whole. Iryna will also focus on the cultural exchange between domestic and international students. Her goals include promoting of the International House and ISO events, advertising mentoring program and working on break-housing issues.
