    • Joy Wang is a sophomore Biochemistry major and Spanish Minor at West Virginia University. During her freshman year at WVU, she joined the Pride of West Virginia Mountaineer Marching Band as a member of the trumpet section. She was also active in the Honors Student Association, the American Chemistry Society’s chapter at WVU, and the internship program of WVU’s Student Government Association. In her free time, she participated in community service activities where she served as the president of the Community Service Club at WVU’s Honors Hall. She has also worked with Habitat for Humanity, the Kanawha/ Charleston Animal Shelter, Thomas Memorial Hospital, and Ruby Memorial Hospital. In her second year at WVU, Joy serves as a Governor on the WVU Student Government Association’s Board of Governors and has worked with WVU Career Services to create a new mentorship program for undergraduate students across campus. She continues to play with the marching band in the trumpet section along with volunteering at Ruby Memorial Hospital’s Pediatric Department. She is also a member of the Pediatric Entertainment Program and Global Medical and Dental Brigades. Joy hopes to attend medical school to become a physician and provide medical care to the underprivileged with Doctors Without Borders.
