Student Engagement

  • Tess McCloud

    Campus Involvement: member of the National Society of Collegiate Scholars

    Leadership Experience: scholarship board of the Exercise Physiology Club, active member in the Helvetia and Chimes Student Honoraries, the community service chair in Alpha Lambda Delta

    tesscloud



    • Tess McCloud is a junior exercise physiology major with an emphasis in aquatic therapy and a minor in psychology. As a graduate of Bluefield High School in Bluefield, W.Va., Tess is proud of her southern roots. As the first Foundation Scholar from Mercer County, she strives to represent the beauty and rich culture found in this area. At West Virginia University, Tess is a member of the Honors College and is actively involved in many clubs and organizations. She is the on the scholarship board of the Exercise Physiology Club, an active member in the Helvetia and Chimes Student Honoraries, the community service chair in Alpha Lambda Delta, and a member of the National Society of Collegiate Scholars. Tess is involved in the local Morgantown community by volunteering with the WVU Exercise Physiology Pump N’ Run, Trunk or Treat, the Rosenbaum House, the Bartlett House, aquatic therapists at HealthSouth and HealthWorks, making and distributing holiday cards to nursing homes, and assembling food baskets for families in need. Tess's favorite WVU Experience was the Summer London Internship Program where she traveled to London for a five-week internship at the Queen Elizabeth Foundation's Neurological Rehabilitation Brain Injury Centre. Tess’ platform involves Enhancing the Student Experience. The purpose of this platform is to inspire students at West Virginia University to get involved in the variety of extracurricular activities to truly help each student make the most of his or her college experience. This will provide students with a support system and help students meet new friends with similar interests. This goal will be achieved by promoting current clubs, organizations, and events throughout campus. Also, students will give feedback about their interests and expectations, so that new programs and activities can be implemented throughout the university.
