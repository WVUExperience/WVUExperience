Presidential and Vice Presidential Candidates


Stephen Scott

Academic Recognition & Springfest

  • Stephen Scott

    Campus Involvement: Honors Student Assocation, Mountaineer Tutoring Network, Chimes

    SGA Experience: Lead the creation of the Catalyst of SGA's Campus Change Scholarship, BOG member 2 years

    ClarenceEXP



    • Stephen Scott is a junior political science and multidisciplinary studies double major, with a concentration in leadership studies, communication studies, and history. Scott is from Shepherdstown, W.Va. Since his enrollment at West Virginia University, Stephen has been actively involved in Student Government Association, where he has served as an intern, governor, and the vice chair. His work in SGA has covered a range of issues including: student support services, diversity and inclusion efforts, academics, advising and retention rates. In addition to SGA, Stephen has been actively involved in other areas around campus, especially the residence halls, where he has served as a member of Student Conduct Board, served as secretary and parliamentarian for the Residence Hall Association and served as a resident assistant. Currently, Stephen is a research volunteer for the Communication Studies Department’s Interaction Lab, the secretary of the Honors Student Association, the secretary of Chimes Junior Honorary, the director of operations of the Mountaineer Tutoring Network, and a Freshman Seminar instructor and a peer leader for the Honors College. Stephen is running on the platform of Academics & Social Engagement. He plans to highlight student achievements during specific college dinners during the Week of Honors. Stephen also plans to bring SpringFest to campus as a way for students to rally together during the spring semester in a positive way.



    Anthony "Braxton" Braxton

    Traumatic Situation Policy & Alumni Connections

  • Braxton

    Campus Involvement: One year as an intern and executive director

    Leadership Experience: WVU Sport and Exercise Psychology Club

    BraxtonDoWork



    • Anthony Braxton is a junior marketing student from South Charleston, W.Va. Since arriving at West Virginia University, Anthony has been involved in several organizations including Student Government Association, where he has served as a governor, an intern and an executive, and the Mountaineer Maniacs, where he serves as an assistant director. While in SGA, Braxton worked on several initiatives from the charging stations found across campus, to the visitation policy in the Residence Halls, where he has worked as a Resident Assistant for three years. Braxton is running on a platform of Well Being & Professional Development. He plans to implement a traumatic student policy that will allow students to stay on course in the event of situations that are out of their control, such as serious illness or deaths in the family. Braxton plans to help students form stronger connections with WVU alumni to help them transition from college into the professional world.
